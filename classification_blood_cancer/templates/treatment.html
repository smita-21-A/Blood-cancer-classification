Treatment Guidelines

Malignant Pre-B Cells

Malignant Pre-B cells indicate *Acute Lymphoblastic Leukemia (ALL)* in an early developmental stage. Treatment involves:

• Induction chemotherapy to remove abnormal Pre-B cells.
• Consolidation therapy to prevent remaining cancer cells from growing.
• Maintenance therapy for long-term control.
• Targeted therapy like TKIs if genetic changes (e.g., BCR-ABL) are present.
• Stem cell transplant in very high-risk patients.

Goal: Achieve complete remission and prevent relapse.

Malignant Pro-B Cells

Malignant Pro-B cells represent a *more immature and aggressive form* of ALL and require rapid treatment. Recommended treatment includes:

• High-intensity chemotherapy to eliminate immature Pro-B cells.
• Immunotherapy (e.g., Blinatumomab or CAR-T) for aggressive cases.
• Targeted therapy if specific mutations are detected.
• Supportive care: blood transfusions and antibiotics.
• Bone marrow transplant for high-risk or relapsed cases.

Goal: Rapid suppression of cancer cells and long-term disease control.

Malignant Early Pre-B Cells

Early Pre-B malignant patterns show abnormal development before full transformation into Pre-B ALL. They require monitoring and early treatment:

• Moderate-intensity chemotherapy depending on progression level.
• Regular bone marrow and blood evaluations.
• Preventive consolidation therapy to block disease advancement.
• Targeted therapy in high-risk genetic conditions.
• Treatment of complications like anemia or infections.

Goal: Stop progression into full malignant leukemia.

Normal Blood Cells

Normal cell detection indicates *no sign of malignancy*. No treatment is needed. However, maintaining healthy blood function is important:

• Routine check-ups every 6–12 months.
• Balanced diet with iron, zinc, and vitamins.
• Proper hydration and good sleep routine.
• Avoiding smoking and high toxin exposure.
• Immediate consultation if unusual symptoms occur.

Goal: Continue healthy lifestyle and routine monitoring.
